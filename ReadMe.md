## Periods

A Period marks a new beat.

Don't mean its the end of a topic, just means that thought has ended, and its being tossed into the void.

---

## Commas

Commans are hard for me? I see them as transitions. 

E.g. if you say "Hey there, Vex"

my brain reads it as "Hey there... Vex" 

because we don't *speak* that way normally. Nor do I. 

I say its a transition, because i still think things like (but or and) require a comma after it, because it transitions into the rest of the sentence/thought. 


## Elipses

Normally an elipses (like in the comma example) is a pretty big pause.

Yeah that don't work for me. 

When i throw an elipses into my text, it ususally is, uh the mental idea of taking a breath? or a re-addjustment. 

## Parenthesis

When I put something in parenthesis, its an internal thought...which is funny because technically its all internal thoughts. 

but its more so a thought within a thought, it stopped me enough to add it. It's sort of like a disclaimer? 

## No Spaces

Sometimes i'll smash all my words together with no spaces. 

somethinglikethisisanexample 

Hard to read usually so i'll give it Pascal Case (SomethingLikeThisIsAnExample) so its easier to parse and read,but usually when i do this

its a means to convey *really* fast talking.

## Emphasis

I usually type emphasis in three ways. 

### Italisize

one is *to give it italisize/or just between two asterisks* this indicates a gape in the speaking, but just enough to notice. think of it like double spaces between words. 

e.g. (this is a sentence)

to

e.g. (`this  is  a sentence`)


but i just write it as: this *is* a sentence.

### Capitals

So when you see me type in all caps, that indicates an increase in volume. not...Yelling perse, but just an increase.

though I think all people see caps as a mental increase in volume ¯\_(ツ)_/¯

### Superscript

So when i type in superscript (ˡᶦᵏᵉ ᵗʰᶦˢ ᵇʸ ᵗʰᵉ ʷᵃʸ) its supposed to indicate like...whispering or soft speaking. 

### Repeating

If i usually type something repeating

It's just my stating the obvious and being very sure, and emphasizing that. 

HOWEVER

if its a BUNCH of periods like "..................................."

That's just my brain going through 39874098273047234 thoughts ats the same time due to ADHD, and me sorting it out...usually.

### Tone

One thing to keep in mind:

The words are not always the tone.

For example:

Sounds like a personal problem.

and

Sounds like a personal problem :3

contain the exact same words.

They are NOT the same sentence.

The emoticon changes how I would say it.

The first is blunt.

The second is playful.

When I use emoticons, they're usually carrying part of the emotional meaning, not just decorating the sentence.
